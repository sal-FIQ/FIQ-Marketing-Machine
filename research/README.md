# Research Directory

The `research` folder is your **source library**. It houses the raw materials and references that inform your foundation files and skills. Keeping research separate from content and instructions helps you trace the origin of insights and update your foundations as new data emerges.

## What to Put Here

* **Customer interviews and transcripts** — recordings, notes, or summaries from conversations with users.
* **Survey results and analytics** — structured data that reveals how people use your product and what they value.
* **Competitive analyses** — documents or notes detailing how competitors position themselves, their strengths and weaknesses, and relevant market trends.
* **Industry reports and articles** — PDFs, clipped articles, or notes that provide context on your market and audience.
* **Internal knowledge** — meeting notes, sales insights, support tickets, or brainstorm documents that surface new objections or opportunities.

You can organize this folder by topic, date, or source (e.g., `research/interviews/`, `research/market/`, `research/competitive/`). It’s better to keep research granular and modular so you can find and cite specific insights when updating your foundation files.

## Keeping Research Tidy

* When you update a foundation file based on new research, note the source (e.g., link or file name) in the foundation file’s “Evidence / source notes” section.
* Review and archive outdated research periodically to avoid clutter. Historical information may still be valuable, so consider a subfolder like `research/archive/` for older materials.
