# Strategy framework reference

Use this file when the request needs extra structure, especially for enterprise, complex B2B, supply chain, transformation, or partner-influenced growth motions.

## 1. Strategy design lens

Pressure-test the strategy through five lenses:

### Revenue fit
- Is the plan clearly tied to pipeline and revenue contribution?
- Does the channel mix fit deal size, sales cycle, and buyer complexity?
- Are the KPI recommendations useful to Sales and leadership, not just marketing?

### Buyer fit
- Is the ICP narrow enough to focus execution?
- Are pain points commercial and operational, not generic?
- Does the messaging match the buyer's maturity and buying trigger?

### Market fit
- Does the strategy match the company's category dynamics?
- Is the plan education-led, differentiation-led, or proof-led for the right reasons?
- Is there a clear point of view on competitors and alternatives?

### Operational fit
- Can this be executed by the likely team size and maturity?
- Are recommendations sequenced sensibly?
- Have major dependencies been surfaced?

### Authority fit
- For enterprise or transformation offerings, does the plan build trust, not just clicks?
- Is there a proof strategy such as case studies, analyst credibility, benchmarks, executive presence, or partner validation?

## 2. Segment prioritization rubric

When segment choices are unclear, evaluate segments on:

- pain intensity
- budget ownership
- strategic urgency
- ease of access
- expected deal size
- sales cycle complexity
- proof availability
- partner leverage

Prioritize segments that combine urgency, commercial upside, and realistic go-to-market reach.

## 3. Messaging architecture pattern

Use this stack:

1. category/problem frame
2. differentiated value proposition
3. pillar messages
4. evidence/proof
5. objection handling
6. call to action by funnel stage

For enterprise motions, make sure each pillar has both strategic and practical proof.

## 4. Demand model selection guide

### Use a focused account-based approach when:
- average contract value is high
- buying committees are complex
- there are named accounts or clear vertical priorities
- sales capacity is limited and precision matters more than volume

### Use a broader demand capture model when:
- the problem is widely understood
- inbound intent exists
- the sales process can absorb more volume
- self-education plays a large role in conversion

### Use a hybrid model when:
- the company needs both strategic account penetration and broader category demand
- some offers are enterprise-led while others support inbound capture
- partners create top-of-funnel leverage

## 5. KPI tiering model

### North star metrics
A small set of commercial outcomes, for example:
- sourced pipeline
- influenced pipeline
- marketing-attributed revenue
- strategic account engagement leading indicators

### Operating metrics
Measures that help run the engine:
- qualified accounts or leads
- conversion by stage
- meeting or opportunity creation rate
- velocity through the funnel
- campaign contribution by channel or program

### Diagnostic metrics
Use for troubleshooting, not as the headline story:
- page conversion rates
- email engagement
- paid media efficiency
- content consumption
- event attendance quality

## 6. 90-day planning pattern

### Days 1-30
- confirm business goals and revenue math
- refine ICP and segment priorities
- tighten positioning and narrative
- audit funnel, reporting, and current demand programs

### Days 31-60
- launch or redesign priority campaigns
- align with Sales on handoff, qualification, and feedback loops
- publish core messaging and content architecture
- implement KPI dashboard and reporting cadence

### Days 61-90
- optimize based on early signal
- expand what is working
- address conversion bottlenecks
- build proof assets and authority plays
- finalize the next-quarter plan

## 7. Common failure modes to avoid

- recommending every channel without prioritization
- confusing activity volume with pipeline contribution
- writing messaging that could apply to any B2B company
- treating brand and demand as unrelated
- skipping Sales alignment in enterprise motions
- promising precise targets with no evidence base
- producing plans that depend on a team larger than likely reality
